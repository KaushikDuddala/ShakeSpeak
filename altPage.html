1. Thou, Thee, Thy:

Explanation: In Shakespearean English, these are archaic pronouns.
How to Understand: "Thou" is used for 'you,' "Thee" for 'you' in the objective case, and "Thy" means 'your.'
2. -eth Verbs:

Explanation: These are archaic verb forms often used for third person singular.
How to Understand: For example, "He walketh" means "He walks."
3. Similes and Metaphors:

Explanation: Common in Shakespearean writing, similes compare using 'like' or 'as,' while metaphors make direct comparisons.
How to Understand: If you see 'like' or 'as,' it's likely a simile; if not, it's a metaphor.
4. Figurative Language:

Overview: Shakespeare employs figurative language to create vivid imagery and convey emotions.
How to Understand: Look for expressions that paint a picture or evoke strong feelings.
5. Elizabethan Expressions:

Overview: Familiarize yourself with common expressions used in Elizabethan times.
How to Understand: Phrases like "By my troth" mean 'I assure you' or "Marry!" expresses surprise.
6. Verb Forms and Pronouns:

Overview: Understand how verbs and pronouns were used differently in Shakespeare's time.
How to Understand: "Art" means 'are,' and "Thou" means 'you.'
